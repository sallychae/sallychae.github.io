Project Description


A form and concept study of a shed design. Using forms and functions derived from the Ely Cathedral's Lady Chapel. Final design is a shed that draws from fundamental natural law that energy is neither created nor destroyed, as employed in the usage of vaults to achieve steeper heights and horizontal spatial gaps in the Ely Lady Cathedral. This study focused on what a shed would look like if its form, structure, and program would be shaped by this physical law: where there is a push, there is a pull. This resulted in a variety of spaces, functional uses, and structures to coexist within one particular shed. Above and below ground. Narrow to wide gaps between columns. Made of a concrete shell and internal vaulting skeleton, the shed appears as though it parts have been grown in part due to an opposing force and in part to fulfill their own purpose. The Final Model is a 1/4"=1' scale, 3D Powder-Printed Sectional model that is nested in a CNC-milled foam base.

[p.1-2] Course Intro & LOs

Focus: Bony orbit anatomy, eyeball layers, aqueous humor dynamics, lens/vitreous physiology, + clinical correlates (AMD, Glaucoma, Cataract, Fractures). [p.1-2]

I. [p.3-5] Bony Orbit & Fractures

  • Anatomy: Paired pyramidal spaces lined w/ periorbita (loosely adherent periosteum, continuous w/ dura). [p.3]
  • Bones (7): Maxilla, Lacrimal, Ethmoid, Sphenoid, Frontal, Zygomatic, Palatine. [p.3]
  • Walls:
    • Superior (Roof): Frontal + Lesser wing of sphenoid; relates to ant cranial fossa. [p.4] Includes supraorbital notch/foramen for V1.
    • Medial: Paper thin (lamina papyracea); relates to ethmoidal/sphenoidal sinuses. [p.4]
    • Inferior (Floor): Maxilla, Zygomatic, Palatine; relates to maxillary sinus. [p.4]
  • Trauma: Blunt force (fist, ball) → Blow-out fracture; most common site = Inferior wall (Floor). [p.4-5]
  • Clin Sx: Diplopia (double vision) + inhibited upward gaze 2° entrapment of Inf Rectus or Inf Oblique. [p.5]
  • Bleeding: Intraorbital hemorrhage → pressure → Exophthalmos (protrusion); trapped eyeball → Enophthalmos. [p.5]

III. [p.8-11] The Eyeball: Layers & Refraction

  • Visual Pathway: Cornea → Aqueous humor → Pupil → Lens → Vitreous humor → Retina (phototransduction). [p.8]
  • Layer 1 (Fibrous): Sclera (post 5/6, shape/rigidity) + Cornea (ant 1/6, avascular, focuses light). [p.9-10]
  • Conjunctiva: Mucous membrane; Palpebral (lines eyelids) vs Bulbar (lines eyeball sclera). [p.22]
  • Layer 2 (Vascular/Uvea): Choroid (nutrients to retina) + Ciliary Body + Iris. [p.9, 11]
  • Layer 3 (Neural): Retina. [p.9] Includes Optic disc (blind spot; no photoreceptors). [p.16]

IV. [p.12-13, 18] Aqueous Humor & Glaucoma

  • MOA: Secreted by ciliary epithelium (lining ciliary processes) → Post chamber → Pupil → Ant chamber. [p.12]
  • Function: Supplies nutrients/O2 to avascular cornea + lens. [p.12]
  • Drainage: Trabecular meshwork → Schlemm's canal (scleral venous sinus) → Venous system. [p.12]
  • Glaucoma: ↑ IOP (> 22 mm Hg) 2° ↓ excretion at TM; leads to optic neuropathy. [p.13]
  • Dx: Fundoscopy shows optic disc "cupping"; Sx = loss of peripheral vision initially. [p.13]
  • Cataract: Opacity/clouding of Lens (aging) → blocks light; Tx = surgical IOL placement. [p.18]

V. [p.14-15] Accommodation & Pupillary Control

  • Near Vision: Ciliary muscle contracts → Zonular fibers relax → Lens becomes rounder/thicker (Accommodation). [p.14]
  • Far Vision: Ciliary muscle relaxes → Zonular fibers tighten → Lens flattens. [p.14]
  • Innervation: Accommodation + Miosis (constriction) = Parasympathetic; Mydriasis (dilation) = Sympathetic. [p.14-15]

VI. [p.16-17, 19] Neural Layer & AMD

  • Photoreceptors: Rods (night) + Cones (color/acuity). [p.16]
  • Macula Lutea: Contains Fovea (highest cone concentration; most acute vision). [p.16]
  • AMD: Most common cause of blindness in pts >60; results in loss of central vision. [p.17]
  • Types: Dry (90%, drusen/slow degeneration) vs Wet (10%, neovascularization/hemorrhage). [p.17]
  • Vitreous Humor: Gel substance; aging → detachment → risk of retinal tears/detachment. [p.19]

VII. [p.20-24] Accessory Organs

  • Eyelid: Tarsal plate provides rigidity; contains Meibomian (Tarsal) glands (secretes lipid layer to ↓ tear evaporation). [p.21]
  • Muscles: Orbicularis oculi (closes eye; CN VII); Levator palpebrae (opens eye; CN III). [p.20-21]
  • Lacrimal Flow: Gland (sup-lat) → Puncta (medial) → Canaliculi → Lacrimal sac → Nasolacrimal duct → Inf Meatus of nasal cavity. [p.24]

VIII. [p.25-29] Extraocular Muscles (EOM)

  • Innervation Rule: LR6, SO4, all others 3. [p.26]
  • Testing Logic: To align muscle axis w/ optical axis for isolation:
    • Recti: Tested when eye is Abducted. [p.29]
    • Obliques: Tested when eye is Adducted. [p.29]
  • Primary Actions (Neutral): MR = adduct; LR = abduct; SR / IO = elevate; IR / SO = depress. [p.27]

Rapid Fire Info

Maxillary sinus = Blow-out fracture floor.
V1 (Ophthalmic) = Supraorbital notch contents.
Bulbar = Conjunctiva lining the eyeball.
Optic disc = Blind spot (no rods/cones).
Lipid = Meibomian gland secretion.
Adducted = Position to test Oblique muscles.
CN IV = Superior Oblique innervation.
Ciliary epithelium = Secretes Aqueous Humor.

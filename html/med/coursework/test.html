I. [p.5-6] The Cervical Scaffolding

VertebraHigh-Yield Board "Must-Knows"
Cv1 (Atlas)Ring-shaped; No body + no spinous process [p.6]. Articulates with occiput via atlanto-occipital joint (50% of flexion/extension) [p.6].
Cv2 (Axis)Features the Dens (Odontoid process) [p.5, 6], which is a remnant of the atlas body [p.6]. Articulates via atlantoaxial joint (50% of cervical rotation) [p.6].
Cv3-Cv6 (Typical)Short bifid spinous processes + transverse foramen for vertebral artery passage [p.5].
Cv7 (Prominens)Long spinous process [p.5]. Transverse foramen is small and does NOT contain the vertebral artery [p.5].

II. [p.7-9] Surface Landmarks + Fascial Sheets

  • Palpable Landmarks: Hyoid bone (angle of neck) [p.7]; Thyroid cartilage (laryngeal prominence/Adam's apple) [p.7]; Cricoid Cartilage at level of Cv6 [p.7].
  • Superficial Fascia: Contains the Platysma [p.8]. Also contains transverse cervical (C2-3) and supraclavicular (C3-4) cutaneous nerves [p.8, 13].
  • Deep Fascia Layers [p.9]:
    1. Investing: Encloses entire neck [p.9]; forms roof of posterior triangle [p.11, 13].
    2. Prevertebral: Encloses vertebral column + associated muscles (floor of post. triangle) [p.9, 11].
    3. Pretracheal: Encloses visceral compartment [p.9].
    4. Carotid Sheaths: Enclose vascular compartments (Carotid a., IJV, Vagus n.) [p.9, 28].

III. [p.10-15] The Triangles: Boundaries + CN XI

  • Sternocleidomastoid (SCM): Originates at manubrium + medial 1/3 clavicle [p.10]; inserts at mastoid process [p.10]. Bilateral contraction → head forward; Unilateral → contralateral rotation [p.10].
  • Torticollis: SCM shortening (congenital) or spasm (spasmodic) [p.10].
  • Posterior Triangle: Boundaries = Post. SCM + Ant. Trapezius + Mid 1/3 Clavicle [p.11]. Subdivided by inferior belly of omohyoid into occipital and subclavian triangles [p.11].
  • CN XI (Accessory): Supplies SCM + Trapezius [p.14]. Injury → inability to shrug shoulder + difficulty abducting arm above horizontal plane [p.14].
  • EJV: Union of Post. Retromandibular + Post. Auricular [p.12]. Descends across SCM to drain into subclavian vein [p.12].

IV. [p.16-23] Anterior Triangle Subdivisions

Sub-TriangleHY Contents / Clinical Dx
SubmentalBound by hyoid + Ant. Digastric + midline [p.17, 18]. Floor = Mylohyoid (CN V) [p.18]. Contains submental lymph nodes [p.18].
SubmandibularBound by mandible + Ant. and Post. Digastric [p.17, 19]. Floor = Mylohyoid (CN V) + Hyoglossus (CN XII) [p.19]. Contains Submandibular gland, Facial a/v, and hypoglossal nerve [p.19].
CarotidBound by Post. Digastric + SCM + Sup. Omohyoid [p.17, 24]. Major contents: Carotid bifurcation, IJV, Vagus n. [p.24], and Cervical sympathetic trunk [p.29].
MuscularBound by midline + SCM + Sup. Omohyoid [p.17, 20]. Contains Infrahyoid muscles (innervated by Ansa cervicalis except Thyrohyoid) + Thyroid gland [p.20, 21].

V. [p.24-32] Vascular & Neural Pathways

  • Carotid Bifurcation: Occurs near upper border of thyroid cartilage [p.25]. Carotid Sinus is a Baroreceptor [p.25]. Carotid Body is a Chemoreceptor [p.25]. Both innervated by CN IX [p.25].
  • Thyroid Gland: Isthmus across tracheal rings 2-4 [p.21]. Blood supply: Sup. Thyroid (ext. carotid) + Inf. Thyroid (thyrocervical trunk) [p.22]. Thyroid ima artery present 10% of time [p.22].
  • IJV: Continuation of sigmoid sinus [p.27]. Joins subclavian to form brachiocephalic vein [p.27]. Distends if venous return to right atrium is blocked (e.g., cardiac tamponade) [p.27].
  • Cervical Sympathetic Trunk: Contains Superior, Middle, Inferior ganglia [p.29]. Inferior + T1 ganglion = Stellate ganglion [p.30]. Postganglionic fibers to face sweat glands travel via External Carotid [p.29].
  • Phrenic Nerve: Derived from C3-C5 [p.13, 32]. Irritation → referred pain to shoulder area [p.13].

VI. [p.33-36] Root of the Neck: Subclavian Artery

  • Boundaries: Inferior boundary (sup. thoracic aperture) = body of Tv1, 1st rib, and manubrium [p.33].
  • Subclavian Artery Parts: Divided by Anterior Scalene m. [p.34].
    1. 1st Part (Medial): Vertebral a., Internal Thoracic a., Thyrocervical trunk [p.35].
    2. 2nd Part (Posterior): Costocervical trunk [p.36].
    3. 3rd Part (Lateral): Dorsal Scapular a. (if present) [p.36]. Becomes axillary artery distal to first rib [p.15, 34].

Anki Quick-Copy: Rapid Fire Strings

5. Subclavian artery part medial to Ant. Scalene is the 1st part [p.34, 35].

7. Thyrohyoid muscle is supplied directly by anterior rami of C1 [p.20, 31].

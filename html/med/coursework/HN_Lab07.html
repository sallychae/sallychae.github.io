LAB 7: ORBIT DISSECTION + SKULL DISARTICULATION

I. [p.1-4] SUPERIOR APPROACH TO THE ORBIT

Step 1: Osteotomy of the Anterior Cranial Fossa

  • Strip dura mater off floor of anterior cranial fossa [p.1].
  • Chip away orbital plate of frontal bone (roof of orbit) [p.1].
  • PRO-TIP: Use one corner of a wide chisel blade for better control [p.1].
  • DO NOT remove the orbital margin [p.1].
  • Identify membranous mucoperiosteum of frontal sinus if broken [p.1].
  • Remove roof of superior orbital fissure (lesser wing of sphenoid) while protecting structures with a probe [p.1].

Step 2: Exposure of Superficial Nerves

  • Incise periorbita (sleeve) longitudinally along the orbital axis [p.2].
  • Clean frontal n. (V1) as it passes superior to levator palpebrae superioris m. [p.2].
  • Trace Frontal n. to its branches: supraorbital n. (lateral) and supratrochlear n. (medial) [p.2].
  • Find lacrimal n. (V1) passing above lateral rectus m. toward the lacrimal gland [p.2].
  • Clean supraorbital and lacrimal arteries near corresponding nerves [p.2].

Step 3: Superior Muscle & Nerve Layer

  • Clean superior oblique m. on the medial side of the orbit [p.2].
  • Identify trochlear n. (CN IV) entering the muscle's upper surface near its proximal attachment [p.2].
  • Trace superior oblique tendon as it hooks around the trochlea [p.2].
  • Cut frontal n. near apex and reflect forward [p.3].
  • Cut levator palpebrae superioris and superior rectus mm.; look for superior branch of CN III piercing them [p.3].

Step 4: Deep Neurovasculature & Ciliary Ganglion

  • Identify abducent n. (CN VI) on the medial surface of lateral rectus m. [p.3].
  • Find nasociliary n. and ophthalmic a. crossing medially above optic n. (CN II) [p.3].
  • Locate ciliary ganglion (pinhead size) between optic n. and lateral rectus [p.4].
  • Identify the parasympathetic (motor) root to the ganglion from the inferior branch of CN III [p.4].
  • Note the common tendinous ring (anulus tendineus) at the apex [p.4].

II. [p.4-5] ANTERIOR APPROACH TO THE ORBIT

  • Bluntly dissect through orbicularis oculi and orbital septum [p.4].
  • Find attachment of levator palpebrae superioris to superior tarsal plate [p.4].
  • Identify the palpebral portion of the lacrimal gland descending into the upper eyelid [p.4].
  • Follow inferior oblique m. as it passes laterally and posteriorly inferior to the inferior rectus [p.5].
  • Expose lacrimal sac and probe into nasolacrimal duct (drains to inferior meatus) [p.5].

III. [p.5-7] DISARTICULATION OF THE SKULL

Structure to Cut Anatomical Details Board / Clinical Note
Cruciform Ligament Transverse lig. of atlas + superior/inferior bands [p.6]. Laxity in Down's syndrome or RA poses subluxation risk [p.6].
Alar Ligaments Upper dens to medial side of occipital condyles [p.6]. Prevents excessive rotation at atlantoaxial joints [p.6].
Tectorial Membrane Superior continuation of Posterior Longitudinal Ligament (PLL) [p.6]. Covers the cruciform ligament posteriorly [p.6].
Apical Ligament Apex of dens to anterior margin of foramen magnum [p.6]. Slender connective tissue band [p.6].

Procedure Summary

  1. Supine: Place hands in retropharyngeal space (between buccopharyngeal and prevertebral fasciae) [p.5].
  2. Seated: Cut remaining muscles (trapezius, longissimus capitis) close to the skull [p.5].
  3. Insert scalpel horizontally between foramen magnum and anterior arch of atlas [p.6].
  4. Use chisel + mallet against the posterior surface of the atlanto-occipital joint [p.6].
  5. Separate head and pull cervical viscera forward [p.7].

Rapid Fire High-Yield Facts

1. Trochlear (CN IV) nerve innervates Superior Oblique m. [p.2].
2. Abducent (CN VI) nerve innervates Lateral Rectus m. [p.3].
3. Oculomotor (CN III) provides parasympathetics to ciliary ganglion [p.4].
4. Nasolacrimal duct drains into the inferior meatus [p.5].
5. Common tendinous ring surrounds optic canal + part of superior orbital fissure [p.4].
6. Alar ligaments limit head rotation; Transverse ligament prevents subluxation [p.6].

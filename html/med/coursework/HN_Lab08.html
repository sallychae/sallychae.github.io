Lab 8: Parapharyngeal Space, Nasal Cavity, + Pterygopalatine Fossa

I. [p.1] Prevertebral Region + Cervical Joints

  • Study of atlanto-occipital + atlantoaxial joints facilitated by skeleton reference [p.1].
  • Median atlantoaxial joint: Articulation between atlas (C1), transverse ligament of atlas, + dens of axis (C2) [p.1].
  • Rotation of head involves both median + lateral atlantoaxial joints [p.1].
  • Prevertebral fascia covers longus capitis + longus colli muscles [p.1].
  • Longus colli attaches superiorly to the anterior tubercle of atlas [p.1].

II. [p.2-3] Parapharyngeal Space + Neurovascular Structures

  • Parapharyngeal space: Potential space from skull base to hyoid bone [p.2].
  • Contents: Fat, CN IX-XII, IJV, + carotid artery [p.2].
  • Carotid sinus: Dilation of 1st part of internal carotid a. (ICA) [p.2].
  • Carotid sinus nerve: Branch of CN IX providing GVA innervation to sinus/body [p.2].
  • ICA typically has zero (0) branches in the neck [p.2].
  • Ascending pharyngeal a.: Branches from medial surface of ECA [p.2].

Pharyngeal + Laryngeal Innervation

Nerve Functional Fiber Type Target / Function
Pharyngeal branch of X SVE All pharynx muscles (except stylopharyngeus) + soft palate (except tensor veli palatini) [p.2].
Internal laryngeal n. Sensory Pierces thyrohyoid membrane [p.2].
External laryngeal n. Motor Parallels superior thyroid a. to supply cricothyroid m. [p.2].
Recurrent laryngeal n. Motor All intrinsic larynx muscles except cricothyroid [p.3].
CN IX (Glossopharyngeal) Motor/Sensory Winds around + supplies stylopharyngeus m. [p.3]; pharyngeal branch provides sensory fibers to plexus [p.3].

III. [p.3-4] Cranial Nerves XI-XII + Sympathetic Trunk

  • CN XI (Accessory): Leaves jugular foramen; supplies SCM + trapezius [p.3].
  • CN XII (Hypoglossal): Leaves hypoglossal canal; loops below posterior belly of digastric [p.3].
  • Superior cervical ganglion: Just below skull base; provides vasomotor fibers to pharyngeal plexus [p.4].
  • Stellate (Cervicothoracic) ganglion: Located anterior to Cv7 transverse process; present in 82% of cases [p.4].

IV. [p.5-6] Nasal Cavity Anatomy

  • Piriform aperture: Bounded by nasal bones (superior) + maxillae (lateral/inferior) [p.5].
  • Nasal septum: Formed by perpendicular plate of ethmoid, vomer, + septal cartilage [p.5].
  • Olfactory nerves (CN I): Ascend into cribriform plate [p.5].

Nasal Meatus Openings

Meatus / Recess Opening / Contents
Sphenoethmoidal recess Opening of sphenoidal sinus [p.5].
Superior meatus Opening of posterior ethmoidal air cells [p.5].
Middle meatus Bulla ethmoidalis (middle ethmoidal cells) + hiatus semilunaris [p.6].
Infundibulum Frontonasal duct drainage [p.6].
Hiatus semilunaris Ant ethmoidal cells (upper) + maxillary sinus (posterior) [p.6].
Inferior meatus Opening of nasolacrimal duct [p.6].

V. [p.7] Pterygopalatine Fossa

  • Pterygopalatine ganglion: Located immediately lateral to sphenopalatine foramen [p.7].
  • Nerve of pterygoid canal: Passes posterolaterally toward foramen lacerum [p.7].
  • Greater palatine foramen: Located medial to 3rd maxillary molar [p.7].
  • Anterior superior alveolar n./a.: Supply anterior maxillary teeth; branch from infraorbital n./a. [p.6].

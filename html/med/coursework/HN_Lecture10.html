II. [p.5-13] CN III Dynamics & Reflexes [LO2, LO4, LO5]

A. Parasympathetic Pathway (GVE-P)

  • Origin: Edinger-Westphal nucleus (midbrain) [p.5].
  • Synapse: Ciliary ganglion [p.5].
  • Postganglionic Path: Hitchhike on Short ciliary nerves (branches of V1) [p.5].
  • Functions: Sphincter pupillae (miosis) + Ciliary muscle (accommodation/lens thickening) [p.8].

B. Motor Functions (GSE) & Pathology

  • Innervates all EOMs except SO4 (CN IV) and LR6 (CN VI) [p.6].
  • Innervates Levator palpebrae superioris (elevates eyelid) [p.7].
  • CN III Palsy: Presentation = "Down and Out" eye position + Ptosis + Mydriasis (dilated pupil) [p.6, 9].
  • Uncal Herniation: Compress CN III at tentorial notch; GVE-P (pupillary) fibers are superficial and damaged first → fixed dilated pupil [p.10].

III. [p.14-20] CN V (Ophthalmic V1) & Corneal Reflex [LO1, LO3, LO5]

  • V1 Branches: Nasociliary, Frontal, and Lacrimal [p.14].
  • Frontal nerve: Divides into Supraorbital and Supratrochlear nerves; supplies forehead/scalp [p.17].
  • Lacrimal Gland Innervation: Parasympathetics from CN VII (greater petrosal) synapse in pterygopalatine ganglion → hitchhike on V2 (zygomatic) → join V1 (lacrimal nerve) [p.18].

[p.20] Corneal (Blink) Reflex

  • Stimulus: Light touch to cornea (wisp of cotton).
  • Afferent Limb: CN V1 (Ophthalmic) via Long ciliary nerves [p.19].
  • Efferent Limb: CN VII (Facial) via SVE fibers to Orbicularis oculi [p.20].
  • Significance: Tests bilateral response (Direct + Consensual) [p.20].

IV. [p.21-25] Sympathetics & Horner Syndrome [LO4]

  • Pathway: IMLCC (C8-T2) → Superior Cervical Ganglion → ascend via Internal Carotid plexus [p.21].
  • Orbit Entry: Join CN VI in cavernous sinus → join V1 → Long ciliary nerves [p.22].
  • Targets: Dilator pupillae m. + Superior tarsal (Müller's) m. [p.23].
Horner Syndrome Triad: 1. Miosis (denervated dilator), 2. Ptosis (denervated Müller's m.), 3. Anhidrosis [p.24].
High-Yield Causes: Pancoast tumor (lung apex), Carotid artery dissection, or Syringomyelia [p.25].

V. [p.26-28] Orbital Vasculature & Cavernous Sinus [LO6]

  • Ophthalmic Artery: Branch of Internal Carotid; enters via optic canal [p.26].
  • HY Branch: Central retinal artery (1st branch); supplies inner retina [p.26].
  • Ophthalmic Veins: Superior/Inferior veins drain to Cavernous sinus [p.27].
  • Danger Zone: Angular vein is valve-less; communicates facial skin infections to cavernous sinus [p.27].

Rapid Fire Board Review

CN III Palsy: Eye is Down and Out with Ptosis and Mydriasis [p.6].
Pupillary Light Reflex: Afferent = CN II; Efferent = CN III [p.13].
Corneal Reflex: Afferent = CN V1; Efferent = CN VII [p.20].
Horner Syndrome: Interruption of Sympathetics; Triad = Miosis, Ptosis, Anhidrosis [p.24].
Cavernous Sinus Thrombosis: CN VI affected first due to central location [p.28].
Uncal Herniation: Earliest sign is fixed dilated pupil due to superficial GVE-P fibers on CN III [p.10].
Lacrimal parasympathetics: Hitchhike on V2 (Zygomatic) then V1 (Lacrimal nerve) [p.18].
